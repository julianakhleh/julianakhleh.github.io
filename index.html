Julia Nakhleh

jnakhleh@lanl.gov · jnakhleh@asu.edu

I'm a full-time postbac researcher at Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL) with the CCS-7 (Applied Computer Science) and XCP-8 (Computational Physics Verification & Validation) groups. My research focuses on machine learning methodology, interpretability/explainability, and applications for nuclear fusion analysis and high-performance computing.

I got my B.S. in Computer Science from Arizona State University in 2019, along with a B.A. in Spanish. At ASU, I did my undergraduate honors thesis on deep learning for generalized planning with Dr. Siddharth Srivastava's Autonomous Agents and Intelligent Robotics (AAIR) lab. I also performed research on the use of FPGAs for distributed deep learning with the Virtualized Infrastructures, Systems and Applications (VISA) lab at ASU for my senior capstone project.

My research interests are in machine learning theory and algorithm development for applications including (but not limited to) scientific analysis, optimization, high-performance computing, and natural language processing.
