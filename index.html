Julia Nakhleh

jnakhleh@wisc.edu

I'm a first year PhD student in Computer Sciences at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. Previously, I was a post-bac researcher at Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL) with the CCS-7 (Applied Computer Science) and XCP-8 (Computational Physics Verification & Analysis) groups. At LANL, I worked on designing and applying machine learning methods to a variety of problems in physics and high-performance computing. See my CV for more detailed descriptions of my current and past projects.

I got my B.S. in Computer Science from Arizona State University in 2019, along with a B.A. in Spanish. At ASU, I did my undergraduate honors thesis on deep learning for generalized planning with Dr. Siddharth Srivastava's Autonomous Agents and Intelligent Robots (AAIR) lab. I also performed research on the use of FPGAs for distributed deep learning with the Virtualized Infrastructures, Systems and Applications (VISA) lab at ASU for my senior capstone project.

I am broadly interested in the theoretical and statistical foundations of machine learning, particularly as they relate to causal inference, uncertainty quantification, and interpretability/explainability.
